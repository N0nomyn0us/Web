CPU

The CPU is the so called "Brain" of the PC from which everything is being executed. So, having a stable and efficient CPU with multiple cores and a high number of hertz is going to save you a lot of loading time.

GPU

This lovely piece is every Gamer's most important PC component when building a PC...this is simply because without it, you would not be able to play games very good. So the more excpensive it is, usually the better it will be.

RAM

RAM for short, or Random Access Memory is important for any PB build as the system needs it to start up the apps and software. Furthermore, it enables you to have several applications on your PC open at the same time. In terms of gaming, 16GB is completely sufficient.

PSU

The PSU is not to be underestimated when bought, thisis because it needs to be a solid peace, otherwise it is not able to divide the power efficiently and especially enough, to the components and their needs.

Motherboard

The Motherboard is the "playing field" where most things from the PC are being built on.

Cooling System/PC Case Fans

PC Case Fans are very useful to reduce the warmth or heat inside your PC Case, especially when your PC is under a high workload a lot of times as it automatically produces a lot of heat. However, you won't need it necessarily when the PC is not built for gaming or other intensive work.

Cooling System/CPU Fan

The CPU Fan is needed as without it, the CPU will most likely overheat and it does nnot take long. You will hundred percent need a CPU Fan if you at least want the PC to run a few tasks.

Cooling System/CPU AIO

A CPU AIO could be beneficial to someone who is more interested in looks as it most of the times glows somewhere. However, it does not end here, depending on what AIO you are using, it could be better or worse than an Air cooler. Additionally, they could improve noise or raise noise, improve the heat reduction or even increase the heat.
